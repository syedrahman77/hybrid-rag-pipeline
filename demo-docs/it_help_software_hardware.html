Requesting Software & Hardware

Category: IT · Last updated: April 12, 2026 · Owner: Information Technology

This page explains how to request new software, hardware, and system access at Northbridge. All requests go through the IT service desk so that licensing, security, and budget can be tracked.

Software Requests

To request software, open a ticket in the IT Service Portal and select "Software Request" (form IT-F-021). Include the application name, business justification, and your manager's name. Requests for software in the approved catalogue are typically fulfilled within two business days. Software not in the catalogue requires a security review, which can take up to ten business days.

Hardware Requests

Standard equipment — laptops, monitors, docking stations, and peripherals — can be ordered from the standard hardware catalogue. Replacements for broken or end-of-life equipment are prioritized. Non-standard hardware requests require director approval and are evaluated case by case. The standard laptop refresh cycle is every four years.

Access & Permissions

Access to systems and shared drives is granted on a least-privilege basis. Request access through the portal's "Access Request" option, specifying the system and the level of access required. Access requests require approval from the system owner. Access is reviewed quarterly and revoked automatically when an employee changes roles.

Service Levels

The IT service desk responds to standard tickets within one business day. Urgent issues affecting production systems should be raised by phone to the service desk hotline, which is staffed during business hours, with an on-call rotation outside business hours.
